Multi-Image Encryption Scheme Based on Chaotic
Pseudo-Random Signal Generator and DWT Compression
YIDAN XU, SUO GAO, YINGHONG CAO, and JUN MOU, Dalian Polytechnic University,
Dalian, China
To solve the problem of resource consumption and information security during color image transmission,
multiple color images encryption scheme according to a three-dimensional discrete chaotic map with pseudo-
random number signal generator and discrete wavelet transform (DWT) compression is proposed. Firstly,
dynamics of three-dimensional discrete chaotic map is analyzed and found to provide better randomness for
encryption schemes. Next, multiple color images of different sizes are compressed to 1/4 of the original size
after DWT processing. The multiple compressed images are merged into a plaintext cube, and the plaintext
parameters associated with the cube are generated. The generated plaintext parameters are combined with
the chaotic map to form the key. A series of sequences are generated by iteration for image confusion and
diffusion to get cipher images. The final simulation results show that recovered plaintext image is still clearly
visible even with lossy DWT compression. The security analysis results indicate that this scheme has high
level of security protection for color images.
CCS Concepts: • Security and privacy;
Additional Key Words and Phrases: Chaotic map, DWT compression, Multi-image encryption, pseudo-random
number signal generator

Chaotic map has been widely used in the image encryption by virtue of their high nonlinear and
sensitivity to initial conditions [23, 54]. Better randomness makes the image resistant to statistical
attacks. Chaotic map includes continuous chaotic map [9, 20], fractional order chaotic map [33,
45], and discrete chaotic map [3, 36]. Discrete chaotic map is fast in iteration and hence is used
in image encryption [34]. It is used in this article and encrypts images by iteratively generating a
series of chaotic sequences to secure the information of the image.
Long channel transmission time is also a problem that needs to be solved while designing
multi-image encryption schemes. The size of the image data can be reduced with the help of
compression techniques, thus reducing the bandwidth required for transmission. Researchers have
developed a range of compression and encryption schemes around this problem. The compressed
image was encrypted using a chaotic map [5]; Wen et al. divided the image, converted the time
domain to the frequency domain using DCT, and compressed the image extracting the DC and AC
coefficients from the DCT coefficient matrix [47]; Tan et al. used the sparse set of DCT combined
with chaotic map to compress and encrypt [41]. Liao et al. proposed a framework based on facial
muscle movements to address the problem of detecting compressed deepfake videos [31]. Liao
et al. proposed a cascaded network in the wavelet domain, which demonstrates strong capability in
detecting watermark information within this domain. It exhibits sensitivity to both high-frequency
and low-frequency details, thereby providing effective protection for sensitive information [12]. Li
et al. have proposed an algorithm for screen-shooting resistant watermarking with grayscale devia-
tion simulation that outperforms existing methods in both robustness and visual quality [30]. Chen

3.2
NIST Test
The combination of chaotic map and image encryption is to provide pseudo-randomness to the
encryption scheme, which in turn improves security. The pseudo-randomness of pseudo-random
number signal generators is usually tested through NIST. NIST includes 15 test items. The test
result consists of two parts: p-value and pass rate, when the p-value of the test result is greater
than or equal to 0.01 and the pass rate is greater than 96% [5], chaotic sequence is considered to
have passed randomness test. The parameters and initial values are set as (푎, 푏, 푐, 푑, 푘1, 푘2, 푥1, 푦1,

Encryption and Decryption Scheme
4.1
Encryption Scheme
Firstly, multiple color images are input, then three channels (R, G, B) of multiple color images
are compressed using DWT technique, LL sub-bands are retained. Multiple LL sub-bands are
merged into a large cube to form the compressed plaintext cube 퐵. Parameters associated with
the plaintext are generated based on the height of 퐵. These parameters are combined with the
parameters and initial values of the chaotic map, which together form the key. Finally, the cipher
cube 퐷is generated. Encryption flow chart is illustrated in Figure 4.
4.1.1
DWT Compression. DWT is a signal processing technique. In this article, the image is
compressed using DWT. Unlike the traditional Fourier transform, DWT can better analyze signals
with local variations. First, the image is divided into four sub-bands: high-frequency sub-bands LH,
HL, HH, and a low-frequency sub-band LL through DWT, which retains the approximation of the
plaintext image. Then, the images are encrypted using LL. Finally, the image is deconstructed and
reconstructed. This DWT compression process is displayed in Figure 5.

6.3.1
Histogram Analysis. It is a fundamental tool for statistical analysis of images, as it reflects
the frequency distribution of pixel values. Histogram of plaintext image is usually unevenly
distributed, with high concentration of certain pixel values and low frequency of others. This
distributional feature directly exposes the statistical specificities about pixel values. However,
cipher image is nearly uniformly distributed, and no identifiable features appear, which effectively
prevents the leakage of information from the plaintext image.
The histograms are displayed in Figures 12 and 13. They display that attacker cannot obtain any
information of plaintext image through cipher image.
6.3.2
Correlation Analysis. Plaintext image has a high correlation, and attackers can analyze
correlation to obtain information about the image. However, the cipher image has almost no
correlation among adjacent pixels, and attackers cannot get the image information by analyzing its
correlation. In this article, correlation between images is analyzed in three directions, horizontal
(H), vertical (V), and diagonal (D) as shown in Figure 14. The correlation between plaintext images
is 1, while the correlation between cipher images is 0 [60].

7
Conclusion
This article introduces an encryption scheme according to a pseudo-random number signal generator
of three-dimensional discrete chaotic map and DWT compression. Firstly, by analyzing the phase,
LEs, bifurcation diagrams, and SE of three-dimensional discrete chaotic map, it was found that they
have better dynamical behavior and can provide better randomness for encryption schemes. The
color images are compressed by DWT to 1/4 of the plaintext image. Then all compressed images
are merged into a plaintext cube and encrypted in the cube. Finally, the simulation results show a
higher quality of reduction compared to similar compression schemes. The security analysis shows
that the scheme has a bigger key space, can resistant to brute force cracking, and it is resistant to
differential attacks, statistical attacks, noise and shear attacks.
For extra pixels in the cube, the scheme is padded with zeros, which takes up space, and there is
a need to reduce the utilization of the extra space in future encryption schemes to make the new
encryption scheme more robust and less resource-intensive.
